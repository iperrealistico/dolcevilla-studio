We do not believe in using film because it sounds romantic. We use it because certain qualities of film remain physically distinct: grain, tonal roll-off, pace, and the feeling that a frame has been truly made rather than endlessly adjusted.

At the same time, weddings are not an argument about purity. They are moving, emotionally dense, logistically complex days. Digital matters because the day deserves coverage that stays fast, reliable, and complete when light changes or moments accelerate.

That is why one photographer always works with film while the wider coverage remains digitally dependable. The point is not balance for its own sake. The point is to let each medium do the job it does best.
