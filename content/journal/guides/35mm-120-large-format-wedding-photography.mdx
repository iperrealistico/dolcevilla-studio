35mm is the quickest and most instinctive of the three. It suits movement, direct flash, laughter, awkward transitions, and all the human unpredictability that gives a wedding its life.

120 medium format is slower and more dimensional. It is where portraiture, still-life details, architecture, and quiet emotional space start to feel richer and more sculptural.

Large format is the most demanding and the most selective. It is not realistic for everything, and that is exactly why it matters. When the day allows for real stillness, large format can create a frame that feels ceremonial in the best sense.
