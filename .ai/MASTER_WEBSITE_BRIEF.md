1. WHAT THIS WEBSITE IS

This website is the public face of dolcevilla.studio.

It must do three things at the same time:
- attract international couples looking directly for a wedding photographer in Tuscany
- make the brand feel unforgettable, not generic
- convert qualified visitors into inquiries

This is not just a photography portfolio.
This is not a venue website.
This is not a planner website.
This is not a generic luxury wedding template.

This website must feel like entering a world with a clear point of view.

2. WHO WE ARE

dolcevilla.studio is a Tuscany wedding photography studio with a strong identity rooted in a real place: Villa Raffaelli.

Villa Raffaelli is:
- our base
- our creative home
- our source of inspiration
- a historic Renaissance villa in Upper Tuscany
- a place connected to art, atmosphere, family, landscape, beauty, stillness, and authenticity

The villa is not to be presented as a public wedding venue open to everyone.
It is not the main product.
It is not a wedding location marketplace.
It is not something to push commercially.

The villa should be understood as:
- the heart of our visual identity
- the place that shaped our taste and our work
- a private and meaningful world
- a place where families can stay and reconnect
- a place where art can happen
- a place that, only in specific and strict cases, could also become part of a couple’s story

Important:
The website must treat Villa Raffaelli as an exclusive private world, not as a public wedding villa sales page.

3. OUR GEOGRAPHY AND SENSE OF PLACE

We are based in Upper Tuscany near Lucca.

This part of Tuscany matters a lot to the identity of the brand.
We are close to:
- Lucca
- Versilia
- Viareggio
- the marble landscapes of Massa Carrara
- the Cinque Terre side of the region
- Pisa within about one hour
- Florence within about two hours

This is not the most obvious postcard Tuscany.
That is part of the value.
It feels more layered, more real, more private, more surprising.

The website should make clear that we know Tuscany from the inside.
We do not just travel through it for work.
We live it.

8. DESIGN LANGUAGE DIRECTION

The design must not look like a standard wedding site.

The design language should feel:
- bold
- experimental
- premium
- editorial
- immersive
- creative
- cinematic
- clean enough to remain usable
- worthy of being noticed for design quality

Reference direction for the hero:
- giant oversized lettering
- almost screen-filling typography
- moving photography behind it
- image sequence that flashes or swaps without soft dissolves
- a hard-cut image rhythm, like a living contact sheet or flickering editorial canvas
- the wordmark or key title dominating the screen

The home page should feel like a statement.
The first screen should be unforgettable.

9. HERO SYSTEM

Important decision:
The giant oversized hero with moving imagery should be used carefully.

Recommended use:
- Home page: yes, this should be the strongest and boldest version
- Weddings page: yes, but more controlled
- Elopements page: yes, but more intimate
- About page: optional, smaller variant
- SEO landing pages: lighter adaptation, not the full experimental version every time
- Ads landing pages: much simpler and more conversion focused
- Journal articles and story pages: no oversized experimental hero, keep those cleaner

Why:
The dramatic hero should feel special.
If repeated identically on every page, it loses impact and starts hurting clarity.

Home page hero:
- strongest version of the concept
- massive lettering
- full-screen or near full-screen
- image sequence behind logo or key statement
- no heavy decorative transitions
- strong, direct, innovative

Service page hero:
- large typography, but smaller than home
- 1 hero image or a shorter image sequence
- still bold, but easier to read and quicker to understand

SEO landing page hero:
- static or lightly animated
- much more message-led
- clear headline first, visual second

Ads landing page hero:
- clean, focused, direct
- minimal distraction
- no over-designed cinematic intro

10. MOTION PRINCIPLES

Motion should be part of the identity, but always controlled.

Desired motion language:
- images floating upward from below
- layers drifting subtly
- cards entering with intention
- galleries feeling alive
- vertical movement
- staggered reveal
- overlapping image planes
- cinematic scrolling moments
- rhythm, not chaos

Not desired:
- too many effects at once
- slow and soft wedding cliches
- fake luxury parallax everywhere
- animations that delay access to content
- motion that makes text hard to read
- motion that feels trendy but empty

The website should feel alive, but still premium and readable.

11. GALLERY LANGUAGE

Galleries should be a major part of the experience.

They should not feel like standard grids everywhere.

Gallery direction:
- editorial
- layered
- floating
- asymmetric
- immersive
- modern
- slightly futuristic
- motion-aware

Possible gallery behaviors:
- images rise from the bottom edge into view
- cards overlap in depth
- some sections use staggered vertical floating layouts
- selected horizontal strips can move as the user scrolls
- image groups can feel like suspended prints or floating frames
- gallery transitions should be hard-cut, sharp, and clean

Important:
Even if the galleries are experimental, they must still be easy to browse.

We must preserve:
- visual impact
- usability
- clarity
- fast understanding
- direct access to inquiry

- too many sub-services
