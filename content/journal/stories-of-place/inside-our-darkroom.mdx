The darkroom matters because it stops film from becoming a costume. It ties the work back to proof sheets, negatives, chemistry, print thinking, and the physical consequences of exposure choices.

That does not mean every image needs to become a demonstration of process. It means the studio’s relationship to film is grounded in real practice rather than in visual nostalgia.

In that sense, the darkroom is part of the same world as Villa Raffaelli itself: not a spectacle, but an environment that keeps the work honest.
